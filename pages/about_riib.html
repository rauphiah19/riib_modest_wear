RIIB celebrates the intersection of Nigerian craftsmanship, Islamic values, and contemporary style. Every piece tells a story of dignity, cultural pride, and purposeful design.

Our Founder's Journey

A Vision Born from Faith & Fashion

RIIB was founded in 2020 by Rashidat Ibrahim, a Nigerian fashion designer who experienced firsthand the challenge of finding modest clothing that didn't compromise on style or quality. Growing up in Ibadan, she witnessed the incredible craftsmanship of local artisans but saw limited opportunities for them to showcase their talents in the modest fashion space.

"I wanted to create a brand that celebrated both my faith and my Nigerian heritage," Rashidat shares. "RIIB represents the intersection of these two beautiful aspects of my identity—where traditional values meet contemporary design."

What started as a small collection of handcrafted abayas has grown into a movement celebrating modest fashion as sophisticated self-expression. Today, RIIB works with over 30 skilled Nigerian artisans, preserving traditional techniques while creating pieces that resonate with modern Muslim women worldwide.

Rashidat Ibrahim

Founder & Creative Director

Our Journey

From a small workshop in Ibadan to celebrating modest fashion globally—here are the milestones that shaped RIIB.

1

2020

The Beginning

RIIB launched with a debut collection of 15 handcrafted abayas, each telling a story of Nigerian heritage and Islamic values. The collection sold out within two weeks through word-of-mouth and WhatsApp orders.

2

2021

Artisan Partnership Program

Established formal partnerships with 15 skilled Nigerian artisans, creating sustainable employment while preserving traditional embroidery and beadwork techniques. Launched our first Ramadan collection featuring intricate hand-stitched details.

3

2022

National Recognition

Featured in Nigerian Fashion Week and received the "Emerging Modest Fashion Brand" award. Expanded artisan network to 25 craftspeople and introduced our signature Eid collection celebrating Nigerian cultural patterns.

4

2023

Community Impact

Launched the RIIB Artisan Training Program, providing free skills development to 50 young women in Ibadan. Introduced sustainable fabric sourcing practices and achieved carbon-neutral shipping for all Nigerian orders.

5

2024-2025

Global Expansion

Expanded to international shipping while maintaining our commitment to Nigerian craftsmanship. Now working with 30+ artisans and serving customers across West Africa, Europe, and North America. Launched our digital lookbook and WhatsApp commerce platform for personalized shopping experiences.
